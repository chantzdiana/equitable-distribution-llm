JURISDICTION: New York
COURT: Appellate Division
YEAR: 1986

During the marriage, the wife made substantial direct and indirect contributions to the acquisition of marital property and to the husband’s professional career. The husband obtained a medical license during the marriage, which significantly enhanced his earning capacity. Although the license itself is not marital property subject to distribution, the court must consider the wife’s contributions to the development of that enhanced earning capacity in determining an equitable distribution. The duration of the marriage further supports an unequal distribution in favor of the wife.
