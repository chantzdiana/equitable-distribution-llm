JURISDICTION: New York
COURT: Supreme Court
YEAR: 2014

The parties have two minor children, and the wife has been designated the custodial parent. In determining an equitable distribution of the marital residence, the court places significant weight on the custodial parent’s need to occupy the marital home and maintain continuity for the children. While both parties contributed financially during the marriage, the court finds that the housing needs of the custodial parent are the primary equitable consideration.
