JURISDICTION: New York
COURT: Court of Appeals
YEAR: 1999
JUDGE: Levine

Grunfeld v. Grunfeld, 94 N.Y.2d 696 (1999)

This case concerns the proper valuation of a professional license and the distribution of marital property following a long-term marriage.

The parties were married for twenty-five years. The wife contributed significantly to the marital partnership, including supporting the household and assisting in the husband’s career development.

The Court examined the valuation of the husband’s professional license and emphasized the need to avoid duplicative awards when distributing marital property and maintenance.

The trial court focused on the duration of the marriage, the contributions of each spouse to the marital partnership, and the future financial circumstances of the parties. The Court noted that equitable distribution must reflect the economic partnership formed during marriage.

The Court concluded that the wife was entitled to an equitable share of the marital assets, with particular emphasis on her contributions to the husband’s career and the long duration of the marriage.

Judgment modified and affirmed.
