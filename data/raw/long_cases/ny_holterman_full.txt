JURISDICTION: New York
COURT: Court of Appeals
YEAR: 2004
JUDGE: Rosenblatt

Holterman v. Holterman, 3 N.Y.3d 1 (2004)

The Court addresses equitable distribution of marital property where one spouse possesses significantly greater earning capacity.

The parties were married for many years and accumulated substantial marital assets. The wife contributed both economically and domestically to the marital partnership, including supporting the family and assisting in business operations.

The trial court considered the contributions of each spouse, the duration of the marriage, and the future financial circumstances of the parties. The Court emphasized that equitable distribution must reflect fairness based on the economic partnership of marriage.

The Court found that the wife's contributions to the marital partnership were substantial and that the distribution of marital property must account for the disparity in earning capacity between the parties.

Judgment affirmed.
