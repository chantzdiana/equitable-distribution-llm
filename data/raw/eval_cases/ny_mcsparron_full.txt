JURISDICTION: New York
COURT: Court of Appeals
YEAR: 1995
JUDGE: Bellacosa

McSparron v. McSparron, 87 N.Y.2d 275 (1995)

The issue before the Court is whether a professional degree and the enhanced earning capacity it creates constitute marital property subject to equitable distribution.

The parties were married for over twenty years. During the marriage, the wife made substantial direct and indirect contributions to the husband’s professional success, including maintaining the household and raising the children while the husband pursued his legal education and career.

The Court emphasized the importance of recognizing the non-titled spouse’s contributions to marital property, particularly where one spouse’s efforts allowed the other to achieve professional advancement.

The trial court placed significant weight on the wife's contributions to the marital partnership and the duration of the marriage. The Court also considered the future financial circumstances of the parties and the economic disparity resulting from the husband's enhanced earning capacity.

The Court held that enhanced earning capacity attributable to a professional degree earned during marriage is marital property subject to equitable distribution, provided that valuation avoids duplication with other distributable assets.

Judgment affirmed.
