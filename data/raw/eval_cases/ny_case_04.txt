JURISDICTION: New York
COURT: Appellate Division
YEAR: 2020

The primary marital asset is the husband’s closely held business, the valuation of which presents substantial difficulty. The court notes the economic desirability of retaining the business intact and free from interference by the non-titled spouse. In distributing other marital assets, the court considers the liquid and non-liquid character of the property and the tax consequences to each party arising from the proposed distribution.
